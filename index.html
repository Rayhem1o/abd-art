About


Abdulrazzaq Abboud, born in 1958 in Masyaf, Syria, is a distinguished visual artist, archaeological restorer, and educator. With a profound passion for the arts and history, Abboud pursued his studies in World History in Syria, laying a strong foundation for his future endeavors. His expertise spans various disciplines, including visual arts and calligraphy, which he teaches to students of all ages.


Throughout his career, Abboud has worked on numerous restoration projects, focusing on Greek and Roman artifacts as well as Islamic architecture. His notable works include the restoration of Corinthian stone crowns and Islamic mosaics. In addition to his restoration work, Abboud has created several stone sculptures inspired by Syrian culture and environment, as well as figures representing Arab heritage. Furthermore, he has produced numerous oil paintings, which have been exhibited in both Syria and Germany, showcasing his versatile talent and deep connection to his roots.


Due to the ongoing war in Syria, Abboud was forced to flee his homeland in 2016. He now resides in Berlin with his family, continuing his artistic and educational pursuits while contributing to the preservation and appreciation of cultural heritage.
